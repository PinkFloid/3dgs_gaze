### Closest 3

1. **[IntenBot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04585).** Pillar (a) is substantially present: its headset captures gaze and speech, and its motivating command is exactly “Bring me that,” although pointing is normally an additional cue. For pillar (b), its RGB-D-derived ConceptGraph supplies object identities, relations, and locations—including objects outside the user’s field of view—and its physical demonstrations send a robot toward targets outside the meeting room. A hostile reviewer can therefore argue that the claimed information flow already exists, with a scene graph substituted for 3DGS. Pillar (c) defeats that attack: seven-room bringing is performed by a virtual robot, while the physical TurtleBot has no manipulator and only performs inspection or return navigation.

2. **[iBotAssistant](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/26/6/1958).** Pillar (a) is real: wearable gaze disambiguates particular pieces of lumber while speech conveys the requested action. Pillar (c) is also physically demonstrated by a Husky–UR5e that travels to a storage zone, grasps the selected lumber, returns, and delivers it. The fatal gap is pillar (b): gaze produces an AprilTag identity from the wearer’s 2D image, while a conventional navigation map and robot-side tag detection perform localization. There is neither a persistent global semantic instance map nor a demonstrated different-room, never-robot-visible referent.

3. **[Beyond Cybathlon](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12984-026-01932-9).** This is the strongest attack on the map-and-execution half: speech selects an item from a full-home semantic map, TagMap returns its global location, and an ANYmal with an arm performs real household fetch tasks. It therefore supplies much of pillars (b) and (c), including the claimed embodiment. Nevertheless, it has no gaze or deictic-reference mechanism at all, so pillar (a) is absent rather than merely implemented differently. Moreover, manipulation after semantic-map navigation is normally human-controlled under shared autonomy, and the evaluation does not isolate the claimed autonomous cross-room unseen-referent trial.

### Verdict

**CLAIM SURVIVES.** IntenBot is the strongest attempted falsifier, but pillar **(c)—one continuous, physical cross-room grasp-and-return execution by a mobile manipulator—saves the claim**; iBotAssistant conversely fails pillar (b), so this supports only a narrow systems-integration first, not novelty of the individual components. Minimal hardening: “To our knowledge, no prior published system has demonstrated, in one continuous end-to-end physical trial and without pointing or hand selection, a spoken deictic reference resolved solely by wearable gaze through fiducial wearer localization in a persistent global metric 3DGS instance map to an object in another room and absent from the robot’s view during command resolution, followed by autonomous navigation, grasp, return, and delivery by a quadruped-plus-arm mobile manipulator.”
