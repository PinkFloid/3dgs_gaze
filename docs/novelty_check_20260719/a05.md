It therefore reaches physical execution while missing the claim’s defining spatial separation.

2. **[EgoSpot](https://arxiv.org/html/2306.02393v2).** For pillar (a), it combines wearable eye gaze and voice, but voice selects modes or issues primitives rather than supplying action semantics inside a deictic utterance. For pillar (b), the HoloLens intersects gaze with its world mesh and sends anchor-frame coordinates to Spot, yet the target is a geometric point rather than an instance in a persistent semantic map; no out-of-view/different-room condition is documented. For pillar (c), it uniquely has the claimed quadruped-plus-arm embodiment and gaze-directed locomotion. 3. **[MIEL](https://emergentsystemlabstudent.github.io/MIEL/).** For pillar (a), MIEL understands spoken demonstratives such as “that cup,” but obtains deixis from robot-observed skeletal pointing rather than wearable gaze. For pillar (b), candidate object positions and visual features come from a prebuilt semantic map, allowing resolution beyond the current camera image; however, the explicit experimental visibility split primarily concerns whether the user is initially visible. For pillar (c), the platform is a mobile HSR, but the study evaluates target identification and does not execute action plans or fetches. It is therefore the strongest linguistic/map predecessor but leaves both wearable gaze and physical completion open.

### Verdict

CLAIM SURVIVES — In live, publicly indexable evidence through July 19, 2026, I found no single demonstrated system satisfying all three pillars end to end. The nearest works divide the stack among wearable gaze–speech manipulation, allocentric gaze mapping, map-based out-of-view exophora, and mobile map-based execution; none performs a cross-room fetch of a map-grounded instance that remains outside the robot’s view during the interaction. This supports a public-demonstration novelty claim, not an assertion about unpublished systems.
