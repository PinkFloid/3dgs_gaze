### Closest 3

1. **[Glance-Say](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06121).** On (a), it is the closest match: Meta Aria gaze selects the object while speech selects actions including pick, put, and pour. On (b), it constructs complete object point clouds and a topology graph with 3D positions, but this is a local explored workspace rather than a persistent building-global 3DGS instance map; no target is demonstrated as never entering robot view. On (c), execution is by a fixed Franka tabletop arm, with no mobile navigation or cross-room fetch.

2. **[EgoSpot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02393).** On (a), HoloLens eye gaze plus voice is genuinely wearable and hands-free, but voice changes or commits modes while gaze chooses a surface point—not a semantic object named through a deictic phrase. On (b), Azure Spatial Anchors co-localize wearer and Spot and the gaze ray intersects the HoloLens world mesh, but there is no persistent global semantic-instance map or out-of-robot-view referent experiment. On (c), the embodiment is exactly quadruped plus arm, yet the study separately evaluates walking to a target and touching a bottle; it does not demonstrate autonomous grasp-carry, fetch, or a cross-room run.

3. **[HoloSpot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11110).** On (a), it uses HoloLens hand tracking to drag and drop a dollhouse object; voice only exposes interface functions, so there is no wearable-eye deictic grounding. On (b), it is the strongest match: an offline aligned room scan is instance-segmented and shared as object IDs and world coordinates, although it uses point clouds rather than 3DGS and does not establish the never-seen/different-room condition. On (c), Spot autonomously navigates, grasps, carries, drops, and returns, but not as a cross-room gaze-referenced fetch.

### Verdict

CLAIM SURVIVES

As of 19 July 2026, the verified demonstrations divide the three pillars among Glance-Say, EgoSpot, and HoloSpot/3DSMaps/LEGS, but none unites them. I found no working demonstration where wearable eye gaze resolves a spoken deictic referent through a persistent global metric instance map while a robot starting in another room—and not seeing the referent during the interaction—autonomously navigates and fetches it. This is a bounded system-demonstration verdict, not a patentability opinion.
