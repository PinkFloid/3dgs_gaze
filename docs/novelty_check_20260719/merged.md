## Top 5 closest overall

1. **[iBotAssistant](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/26/6/1958).** It covers the broadest end-to-end chain: wearable gaze and speech identify a particular item, and a mobile manipulator retrieves and delivers it. Its main separation from the claim is geometric—the gaze system returns an AprilTag identity rather than resolving a gaze ray through a persistent global 3D instance map—and the reported two-zone lab setup is not a demonstrated cross-room trial.

2. **[Glance-Say](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06121).** It most explicitly implements the claimed interaction semantics: gaze specifies the object while speech specifies the action, backed by object point clouds and a topology graph containing identities and 3D positions. That representation is confined to a fixed Franka’s explored tabletop workspace, so it lacks both the remote out-of-view condition and mobile fetch.

3. **[HoloSpot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11110).** It is the strongest match to the map-and-execution half: a persistent pre-scanned room is segmented into object instances, and Spot navigates, grasps, carries, and places the selected instance. The critical substitution is interaction modality—hand drag-and-drop replaces wearable gaze plus spoken deixis—and its demonstrations remain within one scanned room.

4. **[FAM-HRI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16492).** Meta Aria supplies genuine gaze and speech, with language conveying the task and gaze disambiguating the referent before real manipulation. Its target-transfer step searches the robot’s current RGB-D image, however, rather than retrieving a world coordinate from persistent spatial memory, and execution uses a stationary arm.

5. **[EgoSpot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02393).** Its hardware and geometry are unusually close: wearable gaze becomes a shared-frame 3D point through a HoloLens mesh and spatial anchor, then controls a Spot equipped with an arm. Voice controls modes rather than grounding a spoken deictic object, and the evaluation stops at separate navigation and bottle-touching tasks rather than autonomous cross-room grasp-and-delivery.
