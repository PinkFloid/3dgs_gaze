### Closest 3

**1. [EDITH](https://project-edith.github.io/).** (a) EDITH is the strongest direct overlap: Project Aria supplies measured gaze and speech, and the paper explicitly tests “Give me that driver” and other underspecified demonstratives. Its high-level policy selects a gaze-marked egocentric keyframe and converts the request into an executable subtask. (b) This is image/keyframe-to-policy grounding, not a 6-DoF gaze ray intersecting a persistent metric world map; evaluated referents are in the shared tabletop workspace observed by robot cameras. (c) Execution uses a fixed bimanual Agilex Piper, with no navigation or cross-room fetch.

**2. [HELIOS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.22498).** (a) HELIOS has language but no human wearable-gaze deixis; its “gaze points” are planned robot camera viewpoints. (b) It combines a coarse global 2D map with object-level 3D Gaussians and tests targets hidden from the robot’s initial view by a whiteboard. Those representations are accumulated by the robot during task execution rather than queried as a persistent shared map by a localized wearer, and text names the target. (c) Spot genuinely grasps and places objects, but the hardware experiment is one office-area arrangement, not a different-room fetch.

**3. [BUMBLE](https://robin-lab.cs.utexas.edu/BUMBLE/).** (a) BUMBLE accepts free-form language but has no gaze- or pose-based demonstrative resolver. (b) Its navigation substrate is topological/2D, while object parameters come from current robot RGB-D; no persistent metric object-instance map transfers a human-selected world coordinate. (c) It is the strongest execution match, performing real tasks across rooms and floors and retrieving objects. The target is selected from language and ultimately found in robot perception, not grounded outside its view from a wearable gaze ray.

### Verdict

**CLAIM SURVIVES.** Through 19 July 2026, no verified paper or demo was found that joins wearable-gaze speech deixis, a persistent metric global object-instance map grounding a referent outside the robot’s contemporaneous view, and real cross-room mobile fetch. The literature demonstrates each adjoining pair separately—EDITH/FAM-HRI for gaze deixis, 4D Attention for gaze-to-map association, and OK-Robot/3DSMap/HELIOS/BUMBLE for mapped mobile manipulation—but not the end-to-end conjunction; this is a public-literature assessment, not a patentability opinion.
