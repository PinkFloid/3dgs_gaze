### Closest 3

**1. [Glance-Say](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06121).** It fully satisfies pillar (a): Meta Aria gaze identifies the referent while speech supplies the manipulation action. For pillar (b), it stores object positions in a persistent topology graph, but that graph comes from the fixed robot’s prior exploration of its local tabletop workspace; it is neither a demonstrated global multi-room map nor evidence that the referent remained outside the robot’s view. It fails pillar (c) because the Franka does not navigate, change rooms, or perform a mobile fetch. This is the closest collision with the proposed interaction semantics, but not with the claimed spatial separation.

**2. [Gaze-Guided Semi-Autonomous Quadruped Robot](https://www.research-collection.ethz.ch/bitstreams/e9e9a933-ba40-431d-b72e-4d06ac9893a7/download).** It partially satisfies pillar (a): the Pupil Neon wearer selects a physical target with gaze, although no spoken action or deictic utterance is used. It fails pillar (b) because image matching must first find the selected target in the quadruped’s RGB-D camera; the authors identify a common human-robot map as future work. It partially approaches pillar (c) by autonomously navigating a quadruped to the selected location, but it has no arm and does not retrieve the object. Its future-work list—shared map, manipulator, and retrieval—closely describes the missing pieces of the claim.

**3. [4D Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.03606).** It strongly establishes the mapping technology needed for pillars (a) and (b): wearable gaze is localized in 6 DoF and grounded against a prebuilt metric 3D map, including individual object identities. It does not use a spoken deictic command, however, and does not transfer the selected object to a robot. Pillar (c) is entirely absent because the work performs gaze/attention measurement rather than robot navigation or manipulation. It therefore weakens any claim that global-map wearable-gaze grounding itself is novel, while leaving the integrated cross-room fetch claim intact.

### Verdict

CLAIM SURVIVES — As of July 19, 2026, no verified work found combines all three pillars: spoken wearable-gaze deixis, resolution through a persistent global map while the referent is outside the robot’s view, and executed cross-room mobile fetch. The individual ingredients are established separately—especially by 4D Attention, Glance-Say, and semantic-map mobile manipulation—so the defensible novelty is their demonstrated integration under the explicit out-of-view condition, not gaze-language grounding or map-based manipulation individually.
