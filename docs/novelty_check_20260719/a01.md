### Closest 3

**[Glance-Say](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06121).** For pillar (a), this is the strongest match: Meta Aria supplies wearable gaze, speech supplies the action, and the system explicitly describes the division as gaze specifying objects and speech specifying actions. For pillar (b), its robot-explored object point clouds and topology graph are map-like, but the demonstrated structure is a local tabletop manipulation graph rather than a persistent global multi-room map; no experiment places the selected object outside robot sensing in another room. For pillar (c), a fixed Franka performs local pick, put, pour, and rearrangement actions, with no mobile navigation or cross-room fetch. It is strong prior art against any broader claim of novelty for gaze-speech object manipulation, but not against the full claimed combination.

**[Gaze2Act](https://zuo-kuangji.github.io/Gaze2Act/).** For pillar (a), Meta Aria gaze supplies the object or part while language supplies the high-level objective; a voice keyword activates and changes gaze selection. For pillar (b), first-person gaze is explicitly matched into the current RealSense image on the Unitree G1, so it depends on the referent being represented in robot vision rather than retrieved from a persistent global map; its “unseen” condition means unseen during training. For pillar (c), it demonstrates 16 physical manipulation tasks on a legged humanoid, but none includes locomotion, another room, or mobile fetch. Thus even this unusually strong embodied near-match lacks both the map-mediated out-of-view condition and cross-room execution.

**[SLAM-based Localization of 3D Gaze using a Mobile Eye Tracker](https://jiminpi.github.io/papers/a65-wang.pdf).** For pillar (a), it has a genuinely mobile, head-mounted eye tracker but no speech command or deictic-language fusion. For pillar (b), it is the closest technical precursor: visual-inertial SLAM estimates the wearer’s 6-DoF pose and a Kinect point cloud refines the gaze ray into world-centric coordinates over a roughly 13 m² test area. However, the point cloud has no persistent instance-semantic object representation and is not shown as a map shared with a robot. For pillar (c), no robot is commanded, navigates, grasps, or fetches—the paper presents HRI as a future application.

### Verdict

**CLAIM SURVIVES.** In the verified literature and demonstrations searched through July 19, 2026, no single system combines wearable-gaze deixis, a persistent global instance-level map supporting a referent outside robot view, and autonomous cross-room mobile fetch. The relevant capabilities remain split among local gaze-speech manipulators, world-coordinate 3D-gaze systems without robot execution, and gaze-teleoperated mobile retrieval systems without deictic grounding or shared maps.
